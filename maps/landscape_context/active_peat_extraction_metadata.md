## Description

Four polygons delineating areas of active industrial peat extraction within and
immediately adjacent to the Jaunmārupe project boundary, located in Mārupes novads,
central Latvia. These sites represent the primary direct threat to the peatland
ecosystem through ongoing drainage, mechanical extraction, and surface disturbance.

The polygons were digitised manually from the LVM Ortofoto 2021 aerial imagery
using the Jaunmārupe project boundary and adjacent land use as reference. Active
extraction areas were identified by the characteristic dark bare-peat surface,
extraction machinery trackways, and drainage ditches visible in the orthophoto.

## Relevance to the biodiversity credit application

The active peat extraction areas are included in the project map to document the
degradation pressure context. They demonstrate that the project site sits within
a landscape under active exploitation, strengthening the additionality argument
for restoration and biodiversity credit issuance.
